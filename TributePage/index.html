Biography


Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, known as the “Missile Man of India,”
was one of the most inspiring leaders and scientists in Indian history.
Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
He rose from humble beginnings to become a renowned aerospace scientist.
He was the 11th President of India (2002–2007).
He played a key role in India’s civilian space program and military missile development,
earning him great respect across the nation.
Dr. Kalam was also deeply passionate about education and youth empowerment,
often encouraging students to dream big and work hard to achieve their goals.
His life was a true example of dedication, simplicity, and patriotism.
